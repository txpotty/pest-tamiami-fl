Our Comprehensive Squirrel Removal Process

Getting rid of squirrels requires a systematic and thorough approach. Our method is tailored to the specific situation at your property, ensuring the most effective outcome.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first crucial step in effective squirrel removal is a detailed inspection of your property. Our experienced technicians will meticulously examine suspected entry points, nesting sites, and areas of damage. We look for signs such as gnaw marks, droppings, and tracks to determine the extent of the infestation and the primary access points. Understanding the squirrels' behavior and pathways is essential for successful removal.

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels are incredibly adept at finding small openings to enter buildings. We focus on identifying these vulnerabilities in your roofline, vents, chimneys, and other potential access points. Sealing these areas is a critical part of preventing re-entry after removal.

Assessing the Extent of the Infestation

During the inspection, we assess how many squirrels are present and where they are primarily nesting. This helps us determine the appropriate trapping and removal strategy, ensuring all active squirrels are addressed. Having a clear understanding of the scope of the problem allows us to deploy resources efficiently and effectively.

Humane Trapping and Removal

Our primary goal is to remove squirrels humanely and safely. We utilize humane live traps strategically placed based on our initial assessment. These traps are designed to capture squirrels without causing them harm. Regular monitoring of the traps is essential to ensure captured squirrels are promptly handled.

Setting and Monitoring Live Traps

Our technicians are skilled in setting live traps correctly to maximize capture rates. We use appealing baits to attract the squirrels into the traps. We visit the property regularly to check the traps and handle any trapped animals according to local regulations and best practices for wildlife relocation.

Relocation Procedures

Once a squirrel is captured, we adhere to proper relocation procedures. The goal is to release the animal in a suitable environment far from your property, minimizing the chances of it returning. We prioritize the well-being of the relocated animal and follow all guidelines for safe and ethical release.

Exclusion and Prevention

Removing the squirrels is only part of the solution. Preventing them from returning is equally important. We offer comprehensive exclusion services to seal off entry points and make your property less appealing to squirrels in the future. This involves repairing damaged areas and installing preventative measures.

Sealing Entry Points

We repair or seal identified entry points, such as gaps in the roof, damaged soffits, uncapped chimneys, and openings around pipes or wires. We use durable, squirrel-resistant materials to ensure these areas are secure and prevent future access.

Sanitation and Damage Repair

Squirrels can leave behind significant messes and cause substantial damage. Our services can include cleaning up nesting materials, droppings, and other debris. We can also assess and recommend repairs for damage caused by squirrels, such as chewed-up insulation or wiring.

Signs You Might Need Squirrel Removal in Tamiami, FL

Recognizing the early signs of a squirrel infestation is key to addressing the problem before it escalates. If you're noticing suspicious activity or unusual occurrences around your Tamiami home or business, it might be time to call in the professionals for squirrel removal. Being aware of these indicators can save you time, money, and stress in the long run. Squirrels are persistent critters, and they'll work hard to find a way into a comfortable space, particularly during colder weather or when looking for a safe spot to nest and raise their young. Their presence is often more noticeable at dawn and dusk when they are most active foraging for food. Keep an eye out for some of these common warning signs.

Hearing Scratching or Scurrying Noises

One of the most common indicators of squirrels in your attic, walls, or ceiling is hearing noises. Squirrels are active animals, and you'll likely hear them moving around, scratching, or even chattering, especially in the early morning or late afternoon. These noises can be particularly alarming and disruptive, making it difficult to relax or sleep. The sounds might vary depending on where the squirrels are located. In the attic, you might hear them running across the floor or through insulation. In the walls, the sounds might be more like persistent scratching or gnawing. If you hear these noises, it's a strong sign that you have unwanted guests. Don't ignore these sounds; they can indicate an active infestation that needs prompt attention to prevent further damage. It's important to distinguish these sounds from those of other pests, like rodents, as the removal methods may differ slightly. However, any persistent unexplained noises in your walls or attic warrant investigation by a professional.

Visible Damage to Your Property

Squirrels don't just occupy your property; they can also cause significant damage. Their natural instinct to gnaw on things to keep their teeth sharp can lead to them chewing on various materials around your home. This damage can be costly to repair and can even pose safety hazards. Look for signs of chewing on wood, fascia boards, eaves, and even electrical wires. Damaged electrical wires are a serious fire hazard and should be addressed immediately by a qualified electrician after the squirrels are removed. You might also notice damage to your vents, particularly roof vents, as squirrels can chew through screens and metal to gain entry. Squirrels will also tear up insulation to create nesting materials, reducing your home's energy efficiency and potentially spreading debris and waste. If you observe any of these types of damage, it's a clear indication of squirrel activity and the need for professional intervention. Addressing this damage promptly is crucial to prevent further structural issues and potential safety risks. Our exclusion services include assessing and repairing this type of damage.

Finding Droppings or Urine Stains

Like any animal, squirrels leave behind waste. Finding squirrel droppings or urine stains in your attic, garage, or other areas indicates their presence. Squirrel droppings are typically small and pellet-shaped. Urine stains can be visible on insulation or wood and may have a strong odor. These waste materials can be unsanitary and can attract other pests. The presence of droppings and urine also indicates an active and potentially long-term infestation. Cleaning up these areas is important for hygiene and to eliminate lingering odors that might attract more squirrels or other pests. Our sanitation services are designed to thoroughly clean and disinfect areas contaminated by squirrel waste, restoring a healthy environment in your home or business. Dealing with animal waste requires proper protective gear and knowledge, so it's best to leave this task to the professionals.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Exiting Your Home

The most obvious sign of a squirrel problem is actually seeing them enter or exit your home or business. They often use the same entry points repeatedly, so observing their behavior can help identify where they are getting in. You might see them running along power lines to your roof, climbing trees near your house, or disappearing into small openings. This visual confirmation leaves no doubt about the presence of squirrels and the need for removal services. Noticing squirrels carrying nesting materials into your attic or walls is another strong indicator that they are setting up a home inside your property. If you observe this behavior, it's highly likely you have a squirrel infestation. Our inspection process specifically looks for these entry and exit points to develop an effective removal and exclusion plan. Sometimes, spotting a squirrel on your roof or in your yard is normal, but seeing them consistently going in and out of your building is a definite cause for concern and warrants investigation.

Unpleasant Odors

A persistent, unpleasant odor in your attic or walls can be a sign of a deceased squirrel or a build-up of urine and droppings. As organic matter decomposes, it produces strong, foul smells. These odors can also attract other pests and make your home uncomfortable. Locating the source of the odor can be challenging, often requiring a thorough inspection of the affected areas. A dead squirrel can attract fleas or bed bugs, adding another layer to your pest problems. Our professional team can help identify the source of these odors, remove any deceased animals, and perform necessary sanitation to eliminate the smell and its cause. Don't try to handle deceased wildlife yourself, as it can be a health hazard. Leave it to the experts who have the proper equipment and knowledge to safely remove and dispose of the animal and disinfect the area. A persistent, localized foul smell in an attic or void space is a strong indicator of animal presence, whether alive or deceased.

Damaged or Displaced Insulation

Squirrels use insulation as nesting material, often tearing it up and displacing it within your attic or walls. If you access your attic and notice insulation that is ripped, matted down, or pushed aside, it's a sign of animal activity. This damage not only indicates a squirrel presence but also reduces the effectiveness of your insulation, leading to higher energy bills. Damaged insulation can also be contaminated with droppings and urine, posing health risks. Repairing or replacing damaged insulation is often necessary after squirrel removal. We can assess the damage to your insulation and recommend appropriate steps for restoration. Integrated pest control often involves not just addressing the pests but also repairing the damage they cause. Addressing damaged insulation is an important part of fully restoring your property after a squirrel infestation. Displaced insulation is a classic sign of nesting activity from various wildlife, including squirrels and raccoons.

Damage to Garden or Landscaping

While our focus is on removing squirrels from your home, their activity in your yard can also indicate a potential for them to move indoors. Squirrels can cause damage to gardens by burying or digging up nuts and seeds, eating fruits and vegetables, and even damaging tree bark. If you're noticing significant squirrel activity and damage in your yard, it increases the likelihood of them attempting to enter your home, especially as seasons change and food sources become scarce. While we don't typically offer solutions for garden damage, understanding their outdoor behavior helps us predict potential indoor issues and identify attractants on your property. Managing your landscaping by trimming branches away from your house and securing potential food sources can help deter squirrels from getting too close. The presence of numerous squirrels in your immediate outdoor environment significantly increases the risk of them seeking shelter inside your home.

Chewed Wires or Pipes

Perhaps one of the most dangerous signs of a squirrel infestation is chewed electrical wires or plumbing pipes. Squirrels' need to chewconstantly means they will gnaw on many different materials, and unfortunately, electrical wires are often accessible in attics and wall voids. Chewed wires are a significant fire hazard and require immediate attention from a qualified electrician after the squirrels are removed. Damaged pipes can lead to leaks and water damage. Identifying this type of damage is critical for the safety of your property. Our technicians are trained to identify potential risks during the inspection process. While we do not perform electrical or plumbing repairs ourselves, we can alert you to potential issues we discover during our assessment, allowing you to address them promptly with the appropriate professionals. The risk of fire due to chewed electrical wiring makes professional squirrel removal a critical safety measure for any homeowner. Addressing this threat is paramount to protecting your property and loved ones. This is why early detection of rodent, or in this case, squirrel activity is so important.

Finding Nests

Squirrels build nests, called dreys, in various locations, including trees and attics. Finding a nest in your attic, chimney, or another part of your home is a definite sign of an active infestation. These nests are often made of leaves, twigs, insulation, and other soft materials. Discovering a nest with young squirrels requires a more delicate approach to ensure the safety of the animals while still addressing the infestation. Our humane removal strategies take into account the presence of nesting and young animals, ensuring a responsible and ethical process. Disturbing a nest with young squirrels can cause the mother to become aggressive, emphasizing the importance of professional handling. Identifying the location and size of the nest is a crucial part of our initial inspection process, allowing us to develop a targeted removal plan. Finding a nest is undeniable proof of a squirrel infestation and requires immediate professional attention.

Changes in Pet Behavior

Your pets, particularly dogs and cats, might react to the presence of squirrels in your home. You might notice them intently staring at walls or ceilings, barking or meowing at seemingly empty spaces, or becoming agitated. Pets can often sense or hear pests that we might not immediately detect. Pay attention to unusual behavior from your pets, as it could be an early warning sign of a squirrel infestation or other pest activity. While not a definitive sign on its own, combined with other indicators, changes in your pet's behavior can point to a potential issue within your home. Your furry companions can sometimes be your best early warning system for pest problems, including squirrels. Trust your pets' instincts; if they're acting strangely, there might be a reason for it that's not immediately obvious to you. This subtle sign, when combined with others, can strengthen the case for a professional inspection.
